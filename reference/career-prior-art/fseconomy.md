# FSEconomy

**URL:** https://www.fseconomy.net/

## Overview

FSEconomy is a long-running persistent virtual economy system for flight simulators where players operate aircraft to move cargo and passengers for profit.

## Core Economy Mechanics

### Fundamental Loop
"Players use aircraft to move virtual passengers or cargo" to earn income.

### Financial Model
- **Revenue Sources**: Income from virtual passengers and cargo
- **Expenses**: 
  - Aircraft rental charges
  - Fuel costs
  - Other operational expenses
- **Goal**: Make more income than associated operating expenses

### Cost-Benefit Decision Making
Players must manage:
- Aircraft capabilities and fuel levels
- Cargo selection and weight limits
- Route planning to avoid running out of fuel mid-journey
- Rental vs. ownership economics

## Fleet Mechanics

### Aircraft Acquisition Models
Players can:
1. **Lease from others** - Rent aircraft from other players
2. **Rent from system** - Automated rental fleet
3. **Own outright** - Purchase aircraft for personal use

### Aircraft State Persistence
- Aircraft location controlled by players
- Fuel levels managed by players
- All aircraft state visible to other players in shared world
- Changes propagate through persistent environment

## Assignment System

### How Jobs Work
The system assigns cargo and passenger loads to aircraft based on:
- Aircraft capabilities
- Available routes
- Player location
- Profit potential

Players then fly these assignments to earn revenue.

## Persistent World Features

- **Player-Driven State**: "Aircraft location and fuel levels, etc. - are controlled by the players"
- **Shared Visibility**: "Other players see changes in these items within the 'game world'"
- **Community Dynamics**: Environment evolves based on community activity
- **Individual Customization**: "Tailor their experience to their individual liking" whether solo, small groups, or organized communities

## Key Design Principles

1. **Decentralized Ownership**: Aircraft owned by players, not system
2. **Cost Realism**: Operational expenses create meaningful decisions
3. **Emergent Economy**: Player actions drive economic state
4. **Flexibility**: Support solo play, small groups, and large organizations
5. **Long-term Persistence**: Established system (appears to be 15+ years old)
